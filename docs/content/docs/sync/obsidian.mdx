## What it does

Retrace reads your Obsidian daily notes and cross-references them with your conversation data. This lets you correlate what you wrote in your journal with what you were discussing at the time.

## Set up the connection

1. Open Retrace and go to **Settings**
2. Click the **Data Sources** tab
3. Click **Obsidian**
4. Select your vault folder (or let Retrace auto-detect it)
5. Choose which daily notes folder to watch (default: `Daily Notes/`)

## What gets imported

- Daily note content (markdown text)
- Frontmatter metadata (dates, tags)
- Timestamps from filenames or frontmatter

## How it works

Retrace watches your daily notes folder for changes. When you create or edit a note, it's automatically indexed. The content is used to:

- Correlate journal entries with conversations happening on the same day
- Provide additional context during reflection sessions with Claude
- Surface connections between your private thoughts and your conversations

## Good to know

- **Read-only**: Retrace never modifies your vault files
- **Frontmatter tags**: Tags in your note frontmatter are used for cross-referencing with conversation topics
- **No export needed**: Retrace reads directly from your vault folder

## Privacy

- Your notes stay on your machine
- Retrace only reads the daily notes folder you specify
- No vault data is sent anywhere
